inTime Music for any time

This was a project at the 2015 Tufts Polyhack: a hackathon which aims to bring together students from a variety of disciplines for 24 hours to solve problems. The app allows for a user to enter in a number of minutes and a spotify playlist. From this playlist, a sublist of the user-specified length (or as close as possible) is created.

DreamcatcherThrow, catch, improve

This project was conducted by myself and a few members of the Tufts Ultimate team in order to help new members get in throwing practice, even if no other team members were available. It works similarly to a tennis ball return: it will launch a frisbee to a user, who will throw it in the net. The caught disc is reloaded, and eventually launched back. This project reached across a variety disciplines, including mechanical engineering, electrical engineering, computer science, and other areas like marketing (for fundraising) and managing large teams.

Foodprint Your diet's carbon footprint

This project was a result of the 2016 Tufts Polyhack. It aimed to calculate the environmental impact (carbon and water) from a user's diet, present a visiualization of this data, and provide ways for a user to reduce their impact. This ultimately did not leave the prototyping stage, but was my first attempt at making a large web project, and all of the challenges that entailed (styling, team revision control, persistient databses, and independent research).
